Link Building Strategies That Still Work in 2024

By Tochukwu Mesigo | Web Developer & SEO Specialist

Backlinks remain one of Google's top ranking factors. Pages with backlinks have an average of 3.8x more search traffic. As an SEO specialist, I've built thousands of quality links. Here are the strategies that still work in 2024.

1. Create Link-Worthy Content (The Foundation)

Nothing works without great content. Create:

  • Original research: Surveys, studies, data analysis
  • Ultimate guides: Comprehensive resources (like this one!)
  • Free tools: Calculators, checklists, templates
  • Infographics: Visual content people want to share

2. Guest Posting (Done Right)

Guest posting still works when done authentically:

  • Find relevant sites in your niche
  • Create valuable, unique content for them
  • Include 1-2 natural links to your site
  • Avoid "write for us" farms (low quality)

3. Broken Link Building

Find broken links on relevant sites and suggest your content as replacement:

  • Use Check My Links Chrome extension
  • Find broken outbound links on relevant sites
  • Reach out with your replacement link
  • Win-win: They fix a broken link, you get a backlink

4. Resource Page Link Building

Many sites have "resources" pages linking to helpful content:

  • Search: "keyword + resources"
  • Find pages linking to similar content
  • Reach out and suggest your resource

Link Building Methods Comparison

Method Difficulty Effectiveness
Guest Posting Medium High
Broken Link Building Easy Medium-High
Resource Pages Medium High
Buying Links Easy ❌ Risky (against guidelines)

5. Digital PR and HARO

Help A Reporters Out (HARO) connects journalists with experts:

  • Sign up for HARO emails
  • Respond to relevant queries quickly
  • Provide valuable quotes/expertise
  • Earn high-authority media backlinks

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does link building take?
A: Expect 3-6 months to see impact. Links need to be discovered and counted by Google. Quality links show results faster than low-quality ones.
Q: Should I focus on quantity or quality?
A: Quality always. One link from Forbes is worth more than 100 from random blogs. Focus on authority and relevance.
