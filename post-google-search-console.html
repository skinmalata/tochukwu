Google Search Console: Complete Setup Guide

By Tochukwu Mesigo | Web Developer & SEO Specialist

Google Search Console is the most important SEO tool you can have. It's completely free and shows exactly how Google sees your site. As an SEO specialist, I use it daily to monitor client websites and identify issues.

Step 1: Add Your Property

  • Go to search.google.com/search-console
  • Click "Add Property"
  • Choose "URL-prefix" or "Domain" property
  • Enter your website URL

Step 2: Verify Ownership

Method Best For
HTML Tag WordPress and CMS sites
DNS Record Domain-wide verification
Google Analytics If already connected

Step 3: Submit Your Sitemap

  • Go to "Sitemaps" in left menu
  • Enter your sitemap URL (typically /sitemap.xml)
  • Click "Submit"
  • Check for errors after submission

Key Reports to Monitor

Performance Report

  • Total clicks, impressions, CTR, average position
  • Top queries (keywords you rank for)
  • Top pages by clicks
  • Performance by country and device

Coverage Report

  • Errors (fix immediately)
  • Valid with warnings (review)
  • Valid pages (healthy)
  • Excluded pages (intentional)

Enhancements

  • Mobile Usability
  • Page Experience (Core Web Vitals)
  • Schema Markup (structured data)

Common Issues and Fixes

Issue How to Fix
404 errors Redirect or restore page
Crawl errors Check robots.txt, server
Manual penalty Follow Google's recovery steps

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I add multiple websites?
A: Yes! You can add unlimited websites to Search Console. Each needs separate verification.
Q: Why are my pages not indexed?
A: Check Coverage report. Common reasons: noindex tag, blocked by robots.txt, or thin content.
