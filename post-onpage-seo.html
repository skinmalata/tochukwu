On-Page SEO: The Complete Checklist

By Tochukwu Mesigo | Web Developer & SEO Specialist

On-page SEO is the foundation of your website's search visibility. While links and authority matter, you can't rank without solid on-page optimization. As an SEO specialist, I've optimized hundreds of pages—here's my complete on-page SEO checklist.

1. Title Tags: Your Most Important Element

  • Keep under 60 characters (what Google shows)
  • Put your primary keyword near the beginning
  • Make it compelling (click-worthy)
  • Example: "WordPress Web Design: Best Practices for 2024 | Tochukwu Mesigo"

2. Meta Descriptions That Get Clicks

  • Keep under 160 characters
  • Include your primary keyword
  • Write compelling copy that encourages clicks
  • Example: "Learn WordPress web design best practices. Discover how to build fast, secure, and SEO-friendly WordPress websites."

3. Heading Structure (H1-H6)

Tag Purpose Count
H1 Main headline (page title) 1 per page
H2 Major section headings As needed (3-8 typical)
H3 Sub-sections under H2 As needed

4. URL Structure Best Practices

  • Keep URLs short and descriptive
  • Use hyphens to separate words
  • Include your target keyword
  • Example: /wordpress-web-design/ not /p=123

5. Content Optimization

  • Minimum 300 words for informational pages
  • Use your keyword in first 100 words
  • Write for humans, not search engines
  • Use synonyms and related terms (LSI keywords)
  • Break up text with images, lists, and tables

6. Image Optimization for SEO

  • Descriptive filenames: wordpress-speed-optimization.png
  • Alt text with keywords (when natural)
  • Compress images for faster loading
  • Use WebP format for better compression

7. Internal Linking Strategy

  • Link to related content within your site
  • Use descriptive anchor text (not "click here")
  • Create a logical site structure
  • Link from high-authority pages to new pages

On-Page SEO Checklist

  • ✅ Unique title tag with keyword
  • ✅ Compelling meta description
  • ✅ One clear H1 tag
  • ✅ Proper heading hierarchy (H1-H6)
  • ✅ Keyword in first paragraph
  • ✅ Alt text on all images
  • ✅ Internal links to related posts
  • ✅ Fast page load speed
  • ✅ Mobile-friendly design

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does on-page SEO take to work?
A: Changes can be seen in 2-4 weeks for established sites. New sites may take 3-6 months to see significant results.
Q: Can I over-optimize my content?
A: Yes! Keyword stuffing triggers spam filters. Write naturally and focus on user value over keyword density.
