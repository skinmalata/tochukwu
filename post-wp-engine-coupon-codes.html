Common WP Engine Coupon Mistakes to Avoid

Over the years, I have seen people make the same mistakes when trying to use coupon codes. Here is what to watch out for:

1. Using Expired Codes

Many coupon aggregator sites list codes that stopped working months ago. Codes like LAUNCH30, SAVE50, and FREEMONTH have all expired. Always test the code at checkout before getting your hopes up. WPE20 is verified working as of May 2026.

2. Trying to Stack Multiple Codes

WP Engine does not allow stacking multiple coupon codes. You can only apply one promo code per transaction. Do not waste time trying to combine WPE20 with another code—it will not work. What you CAN do is combine a coupon with annual billing, since annual billing is a pricing option, not a coupon.

3. Forgetting to Apply the Code Before Checkout

Once you complete your purchase, you cannot retroactively apply a coupon code. Make absolutely sure you enter the code in the promo field before submitting payment. If you forget, you will need to contact support, and they are not obligated to honor it after the fact.

4. Not Verifying the Discount Applied

After entering the code, always check your order summary to confirm the discount was actually applied. I have seen cases where the code appeared to work but the discount did not reflect in the total. If this happens, try refreshing the page or using a different browser.

5. Assuming the Discount Applies to Renewals

WP Engine coupon codes apply to your first payment only. Your renewal price will be the standard rate. Factor this into your long-term budgeting. This is another reason annual billing with a coupon is the best value—you lock in the discounted rate for a full year.
