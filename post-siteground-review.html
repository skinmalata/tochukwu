SiteGround Review: Why It's the Top Choice for WordPress Beginners

By Tochukwu Mesigo | Web Developer & SEO Specialist

If you're a WordPress beginner looking for reliable hosting, you've probably heard of SiteGround. They're consistently rated as one of the best hosting providers for beginners, and after setting up dozens of client sites on their platform, I can see why.

Let me give you my honest review of SiteGround from a developer's perspective—the good, the not-so-good, and whether it's right for your website.

What Makes SiteGround Special?

SiteGround isn't the cheapest hosting out there, but they've built their reputation on three things: speed, support, and security. They run on Google Cloud Platform, which means enterprise-grade infrastructure at shared hosting prices.

The Good Stuff

1. Incredible Support (Phone + Chat)

This is SiteGround's superpower. Unlike most hosts that only offer chat or ticket support, SiteGround gives you phone support—and they actually answer quickly. I've had responses in under 2 minutes on chat, and phone wait times are typically under 5 minutes.

2. Speed That Impresses

SiteGround uses:

  • Google Cloud Platform infrastructure
  • SuperCacher (their proprietary caching system)
  • NGINX web server
  • PHP 8.2 support

In my tests, WordPress sites on SiteGround typically load in 1.8-2.5 seconds—very respectable for shared hosting.

3. Security That Works

SiteGround includes:

  • Free SSL certificates
  • Daily automatic backups
  • Custom WAF (Web Application Firewall)
  • AI anti-bot system
  • Account isolation (your site won't be affected by neighbors)

4. User-Friendly Interface

SiteGround's custom control panel is much more intuitive than cPanel. Everything is clearly labeled, and they provide helpful tooltips throughout.

The Not-So-Good Stuff

1. Renewal Pricing Is Higher

SiteGround's introductory pricing is great ($2.99/month), but renewal jumps to $14.99/month. This is typical in the hosting industry, but worth knowing upfront.

2. Storage Limits

The StartUp plan only includes 10GB of storage. If you have a media-heavy site, you'll need the GrowBig plan (20GB) or higher.

3. No Email Hosting Included

Like most modern hosts, you'll need to use Google Workspace or another email provider for business email.
