SEO Audit: How to Do One Step-by-Step

By Tochukwu Mesigo | Web Developer & SEO Specialist

An SEO audit reveals why your website isn't ranking. I've audited hundreds of sites and consistently find 10-15 critical issues holding back rankings. Here's my step-by-step audit process.

Step 1: Technical SEO Check

  • Check Google Search Console for errors
  • Run PageSpeed Insights (aim for 90+ mobile)
  • Check robots.txt and XML sitemap
  • Look for broken links and 404 errors
  • Ensure HTTPS is properly configured

Step 2: On-Page SEO Analysis

Element What to Check
Title Tags Unique, under 60 chars, keyword-rich
Meta Descriptions Compelling, under 160 chars
Heading Structure H1-H6 hierarchy correct
Content Quality 300+ words, keyword-optimized

Step 3: Content Audit

  • Identify thin content (under 300 words)
  • Find duplicate content issues
  • Check for outdated information
  • Analyze content gaps vs competitors
  • Review internal linking structure

Step 4: Backlink Analysis

  • Check total backlinks and referring domains
  • Identify toxic/spammy backlinks
  • Analyze competitor backlink profiles
  • Find new link building opportunities

Step 5: Competitor Analysis

  • Identify keywords they rank for (that you don't)
  • Analyze their content depth and quality
  • Check their backlink sources
  • Look for content gaps you can fill

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does an SEO audit take?
A: A basic audit takes 2-4 hours. A comprehensive audit for large sites can take 10-20 hours.
Q: What's the most common SEO issue?
A: Page speed and missing/duplicate title tags are the most common issues I find in audits.
