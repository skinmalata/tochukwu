Shopify Review: Is It the Best E-commerce Platform for Small Businesses?

By Tochukwu Mesigo | Web Developer & SEO Specialist

Shopify powers over 2 million online stores worldwide. As a web developer who's built dozens of e-commerce sites, I've worked extensively with Shopify. Let me give you my honest review.

Quick answer: Yes, Shopify is the best all-in-one e-commerce platform for small to medium businesses. It's easy to use, secure, and scales well.

The Good Stuff

1. Incredibly Easy to Use

Shopify's interface is intuitive. You can set up a store in a day even with zero technical skills. The dashboard is clean, and everything is where you'd expect it to be.

2. Secure and Reliable

Shopify handles:

  • Payment processing (PCI compliant)
  • SSL certificates (free and automatic)
  • Hosting (99.99% uptime)
  • Security updates (automatic)

You don't have to worry about getting hacked or downtime during sales.

3. Massive App Ecosystem

The Shopify App Store has over 6,000 apps. Need reviews? Email marketing? SEO tools? There's an app for everything.

4. Excellent Support

24/7 support via chat, phone, and email. In my experience, their response times are under 2 minutes on chat.

The Not-So-Good Stuff

1. Transaction Fees

If you don't use Shopify Payments, you'll pay an additional 0.5%-2% transaction fee on every sale. This can add up for high-volume stores.

2. Limited Customization

While themes are customizable, you're still limited by Shopify's Liquid templating language. For truly custom functionality, you may hit walls.

3. Pricing Adds Up

Basic plan is $39/month, but add apps, themes, and transaction fees, and you're easily paying $100+/month.
